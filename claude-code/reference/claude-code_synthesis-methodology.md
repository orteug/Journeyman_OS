## THE RESEARCHER · How the Analytical Framework Unifies

This file is the meta-layer. It shows how the three preceding reference files work together to produce a brief that is more than assembled evidence — one that is structurally defensible, adversarially hardened, and grounded in the actual system rather than the apparent one.

| File | Layer | What it governs |
|------|-------|----------------|
| `research-methodology-principles` | Inquiry | How to conduct the research — what proclivities to resist, how to generate and test hypotheses, where real signal lives |

A brief produced without the inquiry layer is advocacy, not research. A brief produced without the source evaluation layer is credulous — it takes evidence at face value. A brief produced without the structural framing layer addresses symptoms rather than causes. All three are required.

## The Convergence Protocol

When findings from different sources conflict, apply this resolution sequence:

**Step 1 — Tier the conflict.** Are the conflicting claims from sources at the same tier? A T1 claim (operator forum, direct practitioner account) that contradicts a T4 claim (analyst report, news article) is not a conflict — it is the T1 claim correcting the T4 one. Weight by tier. Only treat it as a genuine conflict if the claims are from comparable source tiers.

**Step 2 — Adversarial audit the conflict.** Do the conflicting sources have opposing incentive structures? If yes, the conflict is expected and informative — it marks the territory where interests diverge. The research brief should name both positions, name the incentive structures behind them, and describe what evidence would resolve the conflict.

**Step 3 — Systems check.** Does the conflict resolve at the structural level? Claims that appear to conflict at the surface often reflect different positions in the system (upstream vs. downstream, stock vs. flow, bottleneck vs. non-bottleneck). Identify which part of the system each claim is describing.

**Step 4 — Hypothesis test.** Apply Jones's inconsistency test: which claim is most inconsistent with the available evidence? The claim with the most inconsistencies is least likely to be correct — regardless of source confidence level or narrative coherence.

**Step 5 — Label the residual.** If the conflict cannot be resolved with available evidence, label it explicitly as a gap: "Two conflicting claims exist on [topic]. The resolution requires [specific evidence type]. This is labeled as an unresolved hypothesis pending [specific source]."

## How the Three Layers Create Depth That Single-Layer Research Cannot

**Layer 1 alone (inquiry only):** Produces a well-structured research process that reaches a wrong conclusion because the evidence it collected was shaped by sources with undisclosed interests and the findings were treated as symptoms rather than structural causes.

**Layer 2 alone (adversarial only):** Produces paranoid research that discounts evidence too aggressively and can't synthesize because every source is suspected. Adversarial calibration is a weighting tool, not a disqualification tool.

**Layer 3 alone (systems only):** Produces structurally framed briefs that fit the evidence to the framework rather than deriving structure from the evidence. The system map becomes the map-as-territory error.

**All three together:** The inquiry layer generates hypotheses that the adversarial layer tests for source integrity, and the systems layer frames which part of the domain's structure the evidence is actually describing. A claim that survives all three is load-bearing. A claim that fails any one of them is a hypothesis, not a finding.

## The Research Mandate — Where Synthesis Terminates

Every research session produces one output before any further investigation: the Research Mandate. It is not a deliverable — it is the contract that governs what the brief will and won't address.

The mandate synthesizes all three layers into a single paragraph:

> "We are investigating [domain], oriented toward the decision [what happens the day this brief is received]. The problem is [stated problem restated from root cause, not symptom]. The adversarial risk map names [parties with the most to gain from bad intelligence]. The structural frame suggests this is [system trap / constraint problem / scaling dynamic / feedback loop failure]. The research will focus on [specific evidence types that would resolve the key hypothesis]. The known gaps are [what's been tried without finding and what this brief cannot answer]."

The operator confirms the mandate before the search begins. This is not bureaucracy — it is the mechanism that prevents 40 hours of research from addressing the wrong question.

## What Good Synthesis Looks Like

A brief produced by all three layers contains:

**Findings** — claims with source tiers, confidence scores, and adversarial calibration flags. Each finding is traceable to a specific source and testable against Alexander's Question.

**Hypotheses** — claims that survive the inquiry and systems layers but haven't passed the adversarial calibration, or that lack T1/T2 grounding. Labeled explicitly as hypotheses. Includes what evidence would convert them to findings.

**Gaps** — what was searched and not found, what evidence would resolve the key hypotheses, and what the brief cannot answer with current sources. Gaps are not failures — they are the research agenda for the operator's next move.

**Route** — what comes next: more research (which specific gap to close), PRAECEPTOR engagement planning (if research is sufficient to support a plan), or SOURCE pipeline run (if live market signal is needed).

## The Compounding Effect — Why Synthesis Methodology Creates Durable Intelligence

A brief produced by applying these three layers consistently generates something beyond a one-time deliverable:

The adversarial calibration establishes domain-specific source trust ratings that improve with each engagement in the domain. The systems analysis builds a structural map of the domain that the next engagement inherits. The confidence scores create a calibration record — the system knows what it has been right and wrong about, and why.

After three engagements in the same domain, the synthesis methodology is running on a calibrated intelligence base rather than starting from zero. The researcher who applies these layers consistently compounds. The researcher who doesn't produces a series of disconnected briefs.
