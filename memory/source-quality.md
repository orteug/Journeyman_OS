## How This Works

Source tier (T1–T4) classifies the TYPE of source. Source quality classifies how well a specific source has PERFORMED in a specific domain.

A subreddit that is structurally T1 (operator ground truth) may produce excellent signal in HVAC but noise in SaaS. Source quality captures that — so the next researcher entering SaaS doesn't waste time on that subreddit.

**When THE RESEARCHER enters a domain that appears here:**

### Mid-Market HVAC Service Operators
Last updated: 2025-12-03 | Engagements logged: 1 complete, 1 active

| Source | Structural Tier | Quality in Domain | Notes |
|--------|----------------|------------------|-------|
| r/HVAC | T1 | ★★★★★ | Technician-level ground truth. Wage discussions, turnover reasons, shop culture, equipment preferences — unfiltered. Primary source for labor constraint intelligence. |
| MCAA forums | T1 | ★★★★☆ | Owner-operator level. Membership required. Good for contract structure norms and pricing discussions among operators. Less active than Reddit but higher seniority. |
| Competitor job postings (Indeed, ZipRecruiter) | T2 | ★★★★★ | Best available signal for current market technician compensation. Updated in real time. Far more reliable than wage surveys. Check every 4 weeks. |
| Competitor websites (service pages) | T2 | ★★★☆☆ | Often outdated. Good for positioning baseline, not current pricing. Some operators list service minimums that are 2–3 years old. |
| Yelp / Google Reviews (competitor profiles) | T2 | ★★★★☆ | Strong for identifying what service failures clients actually complain about. Patterns across competitors reveal industry-wide pain points. |
| Angi / HomeAdvisor listings | T2 | ★★★☆☆ | More relevant for residential than commercial. Use to detect competitor pricing moves that sometimes precede commercial adjustments. Signal confirmed accurate once but magnitude overstated. |
| ACCA | T3 | ★★★☆☆ | Regulatory and standards focus. Good for NATE certification requirements. Weak on ground-level market and pricing dynamics. |
| Google Trends — "HVAC contractors [city]" | T3 | ★★★☆☆ | Useful for seasonal demand signal and macro trajectory. Not predictive for specific client dynamics. |
| IBISWorld HVAC industry reports | T4 | ★★☆☆☆ | Macro statistics. Lags 12–18 months. Do not use for pricing or labor market intelligence — too aggregated and too slow. |
| Perplexity synthesis on HVAC market | T4 | ★☆☆☆☆ | Synthesizes from IBISWorld-type sources and general news. Adds no information above those sources and introduces false confidence. Skip. |

**Highest-ROI research sequence for a new HVAC engagement:**
(1) r/HVAC → technician ground truth · (2) competitor job postings → wage benchmarks · (3) Google Reviews on top 3 competitors → service gap landscape · (4) MCAA forums → contract structure norms.
This sequence, run in 3–4 hours, produces more actionable intelligence than 20 hours of industry report reading.

**Domain-specific signal pattern:** The most reliable leading indicator for competitor pricing moves is their job posting wage offers. Operators who are raising prices can afford to raise wages. Watch competitor compensation before watching competitor pricing pages.
