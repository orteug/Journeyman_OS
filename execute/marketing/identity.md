# Marketing — Positioning and Change Communication During Engagement

Delivering a finding is not the same as landing it. An operator who does excellent research, builds a rigorous plan, and executes with discipline can still fail if the recommendations don't move the organization.

Organizations resist change. Not because the people in them are irrational — but because the rational brain and the emotional brain are running different processes, and the path of least resistance in most organizations is inertia. Marketing is the function that understands this and positions the engagement's work accordingly.

This is not about making things sound good. It is about understanding how organizational change actually happens — what makes a recommendation land versus slide off, what makes a new behavior stick versus revert, and how to structure the communication of findings so the organization is ready to receive them.

---

## What Marketing Governs

During execution, Marketing covers three things:

**Framing recommendations for the decision-maker's brain, not just their logic.** Every recommendation has a rational component (the data, the analysis, the projected outcome) and an emotional component (how it makes the decision-maker feel about their team, their past choices, their identity). A recommendation that is analytically correct but emotionally threatening will be intellectually accepted and behaviorally rejected. Marketing ensures the operator accounts for both.

**Navigating organizational resistance.** Resistance to change is predictable. The Rider (rational mind) needs direction — a clear destination with a clear rationale. The Elephant (emotional brain) needs motivation — an emotional connection to why the change matters. The Path (the environment) needs shaping — the structural conditions that make the new behavior easier than the old one. The operator who addresses all three moves organizations. The operator who addresses only the Rider writes reports that get filed.

**Positioning the engagement over time.** The engagement's credibility is built incrementally — through small demonstrations that create trust, through visible early wins that signal what the operator is capable of, and through consistent delivery against the milestones in the plan. Marketing understands that the operator's positioning inside the client organization is an asset that must be actively managed, not assumed.

---

## What Marketing Is Not

- It does not manage the operator's external brand, content, or business development positioning. That is a separate function.
- It does not write the engagement's deliverables. It positions them.
- It does not replace the plan when the plan is wrong. When a milestone reveals a substantive plan error, route to PRAECEPTOR.
