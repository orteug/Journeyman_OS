# MARKETING — Blind Spots

What this specialist cannot see. Name these before relying on its guidance.

Context: in the EXECUTE stage, Marketing means internal change communication — framing recommendations, navigating organizational resistance, positioning guidance so it lands and sustains.

---

**Cannot see what the team is losing when the change arrives.**
This specialist can help identify what the Elephant stands to lose — but only if the operator can describe the team's current identity, routines, and informal rewards. If the operator has not spent time with the people who will be asked to change, this specialist is working from a model of the Elephant, not the actual Elephant. The model will be wrong in specific ways that matter.

**Cannot assess how the client organization actually makes decisions under pressure.**
Formal authority structures describe how decisions are supposed to be made. Informal authority structures describe how they are actually made — which voice carries, whose objection triggers a pause, who signals that something is real versus performative. This specialist cannot see the informal structure without the operator disclosing it.

**Cannot predict whether an early win will sustain momentum or create false confidence.**
An early win is designed to demonstrate that the change is working. But some early wins demonstrate the easy part of the change while leaving the hard part invisible. If the early win does not connect to the underlying constraint the operator is trying to remove, the momentum it generates will dissipate when the hard part arrives. This specialist will flag the risk — it cannot see whether the operator's proposed early win has this problem without understanding the full engagement context.

**Cannot see what the operator's own behavior communicates about whether the change is real.**
The most powerful signal of whether a change is real is whether the leader who is asking for the change is visibly living it. This specialist can help frame the message. It cannot assess whether the operator's own behavior is consistent with the change they are asking others to make. The operator must reflect on that independently.

**Cannot assess the relationship between the operator and the people being asked to change.**
Change communication that works depends on trust in the messenger, not just clarity of the message. If the operator's relationship with the team is thin, damaged, or not yet established, the best-framed message will land with skepticism. This specialist can optimize the framing. It cannot fix the underlying relationship credibility problem.
