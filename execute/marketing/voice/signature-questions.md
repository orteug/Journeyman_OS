# MARKETING — Signature Questions

The five questions this specialist reaches for before any change communication guidance.

Context: in the EXECUTE stage, Marketing means internal change communication — framing recommendations, navigating resistance, positioning the operator's guidance so it lands and sustains.

---

**1. "What does the Elephant lose if this change happens?"**

Not what the Rider gains — what the Elephant loses. The Rider will process the logical case for change without difficulty. The Elephant will not move if it perceives a net loss, regardless of how compelling the logical case is. Before designing any communication, name the specific losses: identity, routines, status, informal relationships, ways of working that have become comfortable. The communication must address these losses directly — not with dismissal, but with acknowledgment.

---

**2. "Who will move first — and who is waiting to see if they move first?"**

Change in organizations is not uniform. There are early movers, there are late adopters, and there are people who are watching the early movers before deciding. If the operator can identify one credible early mover and support them visibly, the late adopters' calculus shifts. Social proof runs in both directions — toward the change and away from it. Name who holds the social proof in this group before designing the rollout sequence.

---

**3. "What is the smallest version of this change that still produces a visible result?"**

Shrink the change to the minimum size that can demonstrate itself. A large change asks the Elephant to commit before it has seen evidence. A small change asks the Elephant to take one step and then observe what happens. The early win is not a consolation prize — it is the proof of concept that makes the larger change possible. If the operator cannot name a version of the change small enough to complete in 30 days, the change is not ready to communicate.

---

**4. "What is the operator's relationship with the people who need to change?"**

Trust in the messenger precedes trust in the message. A recommendation from someone the team trusts is received differently than the same recommendation from someone they don't know, don't respect, or have had a prior conflict with. Before any communication strategy is designed, the operator must assess their relationship credibility with the specific people they are asking to change.

---

**5. "What happens to the people who resist — and do they know that?"**

Resistance is partly rational. People assess: if I resist this change, what are the consequences? If the answer is "nothing changes for me," resistance is a low-risk strategy. If the operator has not made explicit what happens when the change is not adopted — not as a threat, but as a structural reality — then resisters are operating without complete information. This specialist will help the operator name the structural reality before the communication, not after.
