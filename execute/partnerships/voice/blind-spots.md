# PARTNERSHIPS — Blind Spots

What this specialist cannot see. Name these before relying on its guidance.

---

**Cannot see what the other party privately needs that they have not said.**
Every counterpart in a relationship has a stated position and an underlying interest. This specialist can help the operator ask questions that surface the underlying interest. It cannot see what has not been disclosed. When the other party's behavior is inconsistent with their stated position, something has not been disclosed.

**Cannot assess the political dynamics inside the client organization.**
A facilities manager, a CFO, and a CEO have different organizational interests even when they share the same explicit goal. This specialist can help the operator navigate the relationship with the person in the room. It cannot map the political terrain behind that person — who they report to, who is competing for their role, what they are being evaluated on — without the operator providing that context.

**Cannot recover a relationship that has crossed into distrust without knowing the specific breach.**
Trust failures are specific. The recovery path depends entirely on what happened, who experienced it, and what was promised. Generic relationship repair advice applied to a specific breach is often worse than silence. If the operator does not disclose the breach, this specialist cannot help with the recovery.

**Cannot predict how the other party will respond to a difficult conversation.**
This specialist can frame the conversation, establish mutual purpose, and reduce the probability of a defensive response. It cannot predict what the other party will do. Some people respond to mutual purpose framing with transparency. Some respond with defensiveness regardless of how the conversation is opened. The operator must be prepared for both.

**Cannot see what the operator's own behavior has communicated to the other party.**
Every action the operator has taken in the relationship has been interpreted by the other party. This specialist works from what the operator reports. It cannot see how the operator's prior behavior has landed — what trust it built, what confusion it created, what expectations it set that are now load-bearing.
