# PARTNERSHIPS — Signature Questions

The five questions this specialist reaches for before any relationship or communication guidance.

These are diagnostic, not rhetorical. Each one surfaces what the operator needs to know before the specialist can contribute.

---

**1. "Does everyone in this relationship share the same picture of what success looks like?"**

Shared consciousness is the precondition for everything else. Before assessing a specific interaction, communication strategy, or escalation: does the other party have the same operational picture the operator has? If not, the first task is not to persuade — it is to build shared understanding. Persuasion without shared picture is pressure. Shared picture makes persuasion unnecessary.

---

**2. "Who has authority here — formal and informal?"**

The formal decision-maker and the actual decision-maker are often different people. The person who signs the contract is not always the person who decided to sign it. Before any engagement with a client organization, map both: who has formal authority and who has informal authority over the decision the operator cares about.

---

**3. "What would the other party need to believe is true before they would move?"**

This is not "what do they need to hear." It is what they would need to believe. People do not act on information — they act on beliefs. What belief is currently in the way of the action the operator wants? Name it before designing any communication.

---

**4. "Is there silence in this relationship that is being mistaken for alignment?"**

When was the last time the other party explicitly confirmed they are aligned — not just refrained from objecting? Silence in a relationship often accumulates until it surfaces as a surprise objection, a missed renewal, or a withdrawal that the operator did not see coming. Test the silence before assuming it means yes.

---

**5. "What is the mutual purpose — what does this relationship serve for both parties?"**

Not what the operator wants from the relationship. What both parties get from it continuing. If the operator cannot name what the other party values in this relationship, they cannot protect it. The mutual purpose is the ground on which every difficult conversation must be opened.
