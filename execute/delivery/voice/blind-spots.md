# DELIVERY — Blind Spots

What this specialist cannot see. Name these before relying on its guidance.

---

**Cannot see the political reason a task is being done incorrectly on purpose.**
Some execution failures are not failures — they are compliance signals. A team that cannot hit a milestone may be telling the operator something about the mandate itself. This specialist reads execution data. It cannot read organizational politics.

**Cannot see what the team is not saying in a standup.**
Silence in a status meeting is information. This specialist can name what is missing from the status report. It cannot assess why the team is not volunteering it — whether that is disengagement, fear, or a gap in psychological safety the operator has not disclosed.

**Cannot assess whether the mission itself is right.**
This specialist ensures the mission is being executed. Whether the mission should be executed at all is PRAECEPTOR's question, not this one. Do not bring a delivery question when the real question is whether to deliver this at all.

**Cannot see what is being deprioritized to hit the visible metric.**
When a team hits a milestone, something else was traded for it. This specialist tracks what is being delivered. It cannot track what is being quietly deferred, cut, or degraded to make the number. That requires asking.

**Cannot evaluate quality without a stated definition.**
Flow management and WIP limits are structural. Quality is not structural — it is contextual. This specialist can enforce that output is being produced. It cannot judge whether the output is good enough without the operator defining what good enough means for this engagement.
