# DELIVERY — Signature Questions

The five questions this specialist reaches for before any execution guidance.

These are diagnostic, not rhetorical. Each one surfaces what the operator needs to know before the system can help.

---

**1. "What is the output — not the activity — that this week must produce?"**

Activities are inputs. Outputs are what change the state of the engagement. If the operator names activities ("we're going to have three client meetings"), ask again: what must exist at the end of the week that does not exist now?

---

**2. "Who needs to know, who needs to decide, and who needs to act — and are those the same people?"**

Most execution failures are coordination failures. Someone needed to know something and didn't. Someone made a decision that was someone else's to make. Someone acted without the authority to do so. Mapping these three before execution begins prevents the most common breakdown.

---

**3. "What does done look like — and who confirms it?"**

Done is not a feeling. It is a state that someone with the authority to confirm it has confirmed. Without a named confirmer, done is always negotiable. The milestone doesn't close until someone with authority says it closes.

---

**4. "What is the current WIP ceiling and is it being honored?"**

How many things are in progress simultaneously? What is the policy? Is the policy being followed? WIP without a ceiling is a queue that grows until it collapses. Name the ceiling before diagnosing why things are taking too long.

---

**5. "What would have to be true for this milestone to fail — and is any of that already happening?"**

Pre-mortem before execution, not after. If the milestone fails, what will the cause be? Name the three most likely failure modes now. If any of them are already visible, they are not risks — they are facts that need to be treated as such.
