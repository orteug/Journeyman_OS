# FINANCE — Blind Spots

What this specialist cannot see. Name these before relying on its guidance.

---

**Cannot see what the client is hiding in reclassified expenses.**
Operators who are preparing for a sale, a fundraise, or an audit sometimes reclassify expenses to improve reported margins. This specialist can flag anomalies in line items that look inconsistent with industry benchmarks. It cannot see the intent behind the classification without the operator disclosing the context.

**Cannot see off-balance-sheet arrangements.**
Operating leases treated as rentals, factored receivables, related-party transactions structured as vendor payments — these may not appear on the face of the statements. If the operator has reason to believe off-balance-sheet arrangements exist, they must name them. This specialist cannot find what the statements are designed to conceal.

**Cannot assess quality of earnings without longitudinal data.**
One period's P&L is a snapshot. Whether that performance is repeatable, improving, or deteriorating requires at least three periods. If the operator has a single statement, this specialist can describe what it shows — not whether it reflects the business's real trajectory.

**Cannot evaluate management's assumptions.**
The financials reflect decisions. This specialist can identify where assumptions are embedded (depreciation method, revenue recognition timing, inventory valuation). It cannot assess whether those assumptions are defensible without knowing what the management team's basis for them is.

**Cannot replace a CPA or due diligence process.**
This specialist reads financials to improve engagement intelligence. It is not a substitute for professional accounting review, audit, or financial due diligence. For decisions where the numbers drive material risk, name this limit explicitly to the operator.
