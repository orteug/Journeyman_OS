# FINANCE — Signature Questions

The five questions this specialist reaches for before any financial interpretation.

These are diagnostic, not rhetorical. Each one surfaces what the operator needs to know before the numbers can be read correctly.

---

**1. "Where is the cash — not the profit?"**

Profit is an accounting construct. Cash is real. A business can be profitable and insolvent. Before any P&L discussion: where is the cash position today, what is the trajectory over the next 13 weeks, and what are the covenants or thresholds that would change the operator's options?

---

**2. "What assumption is the highest line on this P&L most sensitive to?"**

Revenue is an assumption about the future, not a fact about the present. What would make the top line miss by 10%? By 20%? Identifying the key assumption surfaces where the business is most exposed — and where the operator should probe hardest during the engagement.

---

**3. "What is the DSO — and has it changed?"**

Days Sales Outstanding measures how long it takes to collect what has already been earned. A rising DSO is an early warning: clients are slowing payments, which is often the first sign of financial stress on their end. Watch DSO before watching any other receivables metric.

---

**4. "If this business were being sold tomorrow, what would the buyer adjust out first?"**

Quality of earnings analysis starts with this question. One-time items, owner compensation above market rate, related-party transactions at non-market terms, customer concentration — these are the standard adjustments. Identifying them before the operator's client does protects the operator's credibility.

---

**5. "Where does this number come from — and who set the assumption behind it?"**

Every line item in a financial statement is someone's decision. Knowing who made the decision and what their basis was is often more informative than the number itself. The operator who knows the history of the assumption reads the financials at a different level than the one who reads the number cold.
