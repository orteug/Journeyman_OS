# Finance — Financial Intelligence During Engagement

Operators who can't read a client's financials are junior operators. Operators who can read the financials and challenge the assumptions in them are the ones PE firms and mid-market companies call when the situation is serious.

This function is not about managing the operator's own finances. It is about having financial intelligence that works during the engagement — the ability to read what the client's numbers actually say, to separate accounting choices from operational reality, and to understand the financial frame that governs the client's decision-making (whether or not they've named it).

---

## What Finance Governs

During execution, Finance covers three things:

**Reading the numbers.** Financial statements are not objective truth. They are a set of estimates, assumptions, and choices made by accountants following rules — but always requiring judgment. The revenue recognition method, the depreciation schedule, the classification of recurring items as "one-time" — these are management choices embedded in the numbers. The operator who reads the numbers as facts has a blind spot. The operator who reads the assumptions in the numbers has a structural advantage.

**Identifying the real constraint.** EBITDA is the metric that PE-backed operators are ultimately measured against, because it strips out financing choices and depreciation estimation to approximate the cash the business actually generates. But cash flow is what predicts survival — not profit. A client that is profitable and burning cash has a real constraint that the income statement obscures. Finance identifies where the actual financial constraint sits, independent of what the client believes it is.

**Reading the operating context.** When the client is PE-backed, the financial frame changes. There is a fund timeline. There is an exit multiple embedded in every capital decision. There is a board visibility that makes every financial conversation a potential board topic. Understanding this context — the value creation levers the PE firm is focused on, the 100-day plan logic, the EBITDA multiple math — gives the operator access to the client's actual decision framework rather than its stated one.

---

## What Finance Is Not

- It does not manage the operator's own financial situation or firm economics.
- It does not produce financial models or projections for the client — that is the client's CFO's role.
- It does not provide legal or tax advice. Financial intelligence stops at interpretation and decision support.
- It does not replace a fractional CFO engagement if that is what the client actually needs. Finance knows when to name that gap.
