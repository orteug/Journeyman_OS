# WORKFLOW 1 — BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT
## Find and Win Operator Engagements
**Version:** 1.1 · **Last updated:** 2026-05-30
**Methodology sources:** Maister/Green/Galford (The Trusted Advisor), Consulting Success, SPIN Selling (Rackham), The Challenger Sale (CEB), Grove (High Output Management — Task-Relevant Maturity), Patterson et al. (Crucial Conversations — STATE/shared pool), Slootman (Amp It Up — amplitude, direct communication), Greene (Laws of Human Nature), Parrish (The Great Mental Models — second-order thinking, Occam's Razor)

## Purpose

Build a predictable, self-reinforcing pipeline of fractional operator engagements — without feast-or-famine cycles. Prevent dependency on any single client or channel.

---

## Flywheel Mapping

```
SOURCE     → detects opportunity signals in target markets
FILTER     → THE RESEARCHER investigates prospect before any contact
DEVELOP    → PRAECEPTOR prepares operator for the conversation
EXECUTE    → PARTNERSHIPS outreaches · MARKETING builds inbound
             DELIVERY builds proposals · FINANCE prices engagements
```

---

## The Trust Foundation (Non-Negotiable)

Every tactic in this workflow sits on the Trust Equation (Maister):

```
TRUST = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation
```

- **Credibility** — your expertise is visible and specific. Not "operations consultant." "I help $1M–$5M outdoor recreation operators fix the systems breaking as they scale."
- **Reliability** — you do what you say. On time. Every time. Referrals come from this.
- **Intimacy** — you understand the client's real problem, not the stated one.
- **Self-Orientation** — the lower the better. Clients feel when you're selling vs helping. The moment you pitch before you understand, trust drops.

## Stage 1 — Define Your Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

**Who this is for:** operators who haven't defined — or keep drifting from — their specific client type.

**Method:** Niche clarity before any BD activity. Vague positioning wastes all downstream effort.

**ICP components to define:**

```
COMPANY PROFILE
- Revenue range: $500K–$5M (outgrown startup systems, can't yet afford FT COO)
- Headcount: 5–25 employees
- Stage: post-product-market fit, pre-scalable-ops
- Industry: target verticals (outdoor recreation, FLS/HVAC, service businesses)
- Geography: if relevant

TRIGGER EVENTS (what makes them ready NOW)
- Posting for COO or VP Ops they can't afford full-time
- Owner saying "I can't stop working IN the business"
- Rapid hiring without systems to support it
- Recent acquisition or ownership transition
- Missed delivery deadlines, team conflicts, client churn

DISQUALIFIERS (save time — exit early)
- No budget for operational investment
- Owner not willing to delegate
- Business fundamentally broken (not just operationally messy)
- Timeline under 30 days (no meaningful engagement possible)
```

**OUTPUT:** A one-page ICP document. SOURCE uses this as its opportunity signal criteria. THE RESEARCHER uses this as the fit assessment framework.

## Stage 2 — Signal Detection (SOURCE Layer)

SOURCE runs continuously. For BD, it watches for ICP trigger events.

**SOURCE watchlist criteria for BD:**

```
T1 SIGNALS (highest trust — act immediately)
- Owner-operator forums: posts about operational chaos, team problems, scaling frustration
- LinkedIn: owners asking for COO/ops recommendations
- Warm referrals from existing network

T2 SIGNALS (competitive intelligence — qualify quickly)
- Job postings: COO, VP Operations, Director of Operations for $1M–$5M companies
- Indeed/LinkedIn postings that match ICP
- Competitor fractional operators landing clients in your target verticals

T3 SIGNALS (market structure — context)
- Industry associations reporting scaling trends in target verticals
- M&A activity (acquired businesses almost always need operational cleanup)
- Regulatory changes creating operational compliance needs

T4 SIGNALS (synthesized — awareness only)
- Industry reports on fractional executive demand
- News about growth challenges in target sectors
```

**SOURCE output:** Scored opportunity package → THE RESEARCHER for investigation.

## Stage 3 — Prospect Investigation (FILTER Layer)

THE RESEARCHER investigates every qualified signal before any contact is made.

**Engagement intake for BD:**

```
DOMAIN:   What industry/vertical is this prospect in?
PROBLEM:  What operational trigger is visible? What's the evidence?
SCOPE:    How deep does the problem likely run?
CONTEXT:  What do I already know about this company or owner?
GAPS:     What do I need to find before I can have an intelligent first conversation?
```

**What THE RESEARCHER produces — Prospect Brief:**

```
PROSPECT BRIEF
- Company overview (revenue, headcount, growth stage — T2/T3 sources)
- Owner background (LinkedIn, previous companies, operational experience)
- Visible operational problems (T1 — forums, reviews, job postings)
- Competitive landscape (who else serves this niche operationally)
- ICP fit score (strong / qualified / weak — with reasoning)
- First conversation angle (what problem do you lead with?)
- Questions to ask in discovery (not to pitch — to understand)
- Red flags (disqualifiers to surface early)
- Owner's Task-Relevant Maturity assessment (see Stage 4)
```

**Gate:** If ICP fit score is weak, do not proceed. PRAECEPTOR does not waste time on bad fits.

## Stage 4 — Conversation Preparation (DEVELOP Layer)

PRAECEPTOR receives the prospect brief. Prepares the operator before any contact.

**Owner TRM Assessment (Grove — High Output Management)**

Before any conversation, assess this owner's Task-Relevant Maturity for operational change — their combination of relevant experience and demonstrated willingness to delegate. This determines how the discovery call should be run.

```
LOW TRM OWNER (first-time scaling, no prior COO experience):
→ They don't know what they don't know. Discovery must educate before it diagnoses.
→ Open with framing: "Let me describe what I typically see in businesses at your stage."
→ SPIN questions build the implication before the need-payoff.

MEDIUM TRM OWNER (has tried consultants before, understands the problem exists):
→ They know the problem but haven't solved it. Discovery validates and focuses.
→ Skip the education. Go straight to specifics: "What specifically broke down last time?"

HIGH TRM OWNER (has scaled before, knows what a COO does):
→ They can define the problem themselves. Discovery confirms fit.
→ Lead with outcomes, not process: "What does a successful 90-day engagement look like?"
```

**PRAECEPTOR BD mode prepares:**

```
POSITIONING
- How does the operator frame their value for THIS specific prospect?
- Not a generic pitch. Based on the prospect brief.
- Lead with the problem you can solve for them, not your credentials.

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (SPIN framework — Rackham)
- Situation: "Walk me through how your operations run today."
- Problem: "Where does the team feel the most friction right now?"
- Implication: "What happens to growth if this doesn't get fixed in the next 90 days?"
- Need-Payoff: "What would it mean for you personally if this was running smoothly?"

TRUSTED ADVISOR ORIENTATION (Maister)
- Enter the conversation to understand, not to sell.
- The goal of the first call: earn the right to a second conversation.
- Do not propose on the first call. Ever.
- If they ask for a proposal before you understand the problem, ask one more question.

OBJECTION PREP
- "We've tried consultants before and nothing changed." → "What would have to be different this time for it to stick?"
- "We can't afford a fractional COO." → "What's the cost of not fixing this in the next 6 months?"
- "I need to think about it." → "What specifically would need to be true for this to make sense?"
```

## Stage 5 — Inbound Pipeline (EXECUTE — MARKETING)

Inbound compounds over time. Outbound generates immediate pipeline. Run both simultaneously.

**MARKETING BD function:**

```
THOUGHT LEADERSHIP (long-term — 6–12 month compounding)
- Substack / Journeyman's Journal: document what you see as an operator
- LinkedIn: one post per week minimum — operational insights, not inspirational content
  Format that works: "Here's a problem I see in [niche]. Here's how operators fix it."
- YouTube: process walkthroughs, operator case studies (with permission)
- Every piece answers: "Why is this operator the right person to fix this problem?"

CONTENT STRATEGY
- Write for the owner with 15 employees who is drowning in operational chaos
- Not for other operators. Not for investors. For the buyer.
- Case studies > opinion pieces. Real outcomes > general advice.

POSITIONING SIGNAL
- LinkedIn headline: specific, ICP-targeted. Not "Fractional COO."
  → "I help $1M–$5M service businesses fix the operations breaking their growth."
- Website / bio: lead with the problem you solve, not your background.
- Social proof: every engagement produces a case study or testimonial (with permission).

CHANNEL PRIORITY
- LinkedIn: primary for direct B2B discovery
- Substack: trust-building, pre-qualification
- YouTube: depth and credibility for hesitant buyers
- Give each channel 90 days minimum before evaluating (Consulting Success rule)
```

## Stage 6 — Outbound Pipeline (EXECUTE — PARTNERSHIPS)

Outbound accelerates pipeline. Works in parallel with inbound. Different mechanism, same ICP.

**PARTNERSHIPS BD function:**

```
REFERRAL SYSTEM (highest ROI — 71% better conversion, 69% faster close)
- Referrals do not happen organically at scale. Build a system.
- Referral partners: adjacent service providers to your ICP
  → Accountants, lawyers, business bankers, financial advisors serving SMBs
  → Other fractional executives (CMO, CFO) who don't do ops
  → Business brokers (post-acquisition ops cleanup is a recurring need)
- Formal referral agreement: define terms, track source, reciprocate consistently
- Ask after every successful engagement milestone, not just at close.

DIRECT OUTREACH (LinkedIn — primary channel)
  → Research before contact (THE RESEARCHER brief in hand)
  → Connection request: no pitch. Reference something specific about their business.
  → Message 1 (after connect): observation about their situation. One question. No ask.
  → Message 2 (5–7 days): value first. Share something relevant. Still no ask.
  → Message 3: request a conversation. Specific problem. Specific outcome.
  → If no response after message 3: exit gracefully. Re-engage in 90 days.

WARM OUTREACH (existing network)
  → Past colleagues, clients, employers — they already know your work
  → "I'm taking on two new fractional engagements this quarter in [vertical].
     Know anyone who's been struggling with [specific problem]?"
  → Don't pitch. Ask for referrals and introductions.

SILENT OUTREACH — OCCAM'S RAZOR CHECK (Parrish)
  Before diagnosing why outreach isn't converting, apply the simplest explanation first:
  → "Did the message clearly communicate what I do and what problem I solve?"
  Before assuming sophisticated buyer evaluation, check: did they understand the offer?
  Fix message clarity before building complex qualification frameworks.
```

## Stage 7 — Qualification Gate

**Before investing in discovery, qualify hard. Bad fit burns time neither party gets back.**

```
QUALIFICATION CRITERIA (all five required to proceed)

1. BUDGET SIGNAL
   Do they have budget for operational investment?
   Fractional COO retainer: $3K–$10K/month depending on scope.
   If budget conversation creates immediate objection with no curiosity → exit.

2. DECISION AUTHORITY
   Is the person you're talking to the decision maker?
   If not: who is? Can you get in front of them?

3. OPERATIONAL PAIN
   Is there a real, specific, expensive problem?
   Vague "we want to grow better" is not enough.
   "We've lost three key hires in 90 days because no one knows who does what" is enough.

4. TIMELINE
   Are they ready to move in the next 30–60 days?
   "Someday" is not a timeline. Protect your pipeline for active buyers.

5. COACHABILITY
   Is the owner willing to delegate and change?
   The most expensive engagement is one where the owner won't let you do the work.
   Surface this early: "Tell me about a time you brought in outside help. How did that go?"
```

## Stage 8 — Discovery Call

**Purpose:** Understand the real problem. Earn the right to propose. Do not pitch.

```
CALL STRUCTURE (45–60 minutes)

OPEN (5 min) — Crucial Conversations: Establish Mutual Purpose first
- Set the agenda and name the shared goal:
  "My goal today is to understand what's going on in your operation.
   If there's a fit, we'll talk about what working together looks like.
   If there isn't, I'll tell you honestly. Sound good?"
- Mutual Purpose establishes safety. Without safety, the owner withholds the real problem.
  Watch for silence moves (hedging, redirecting) or violence moves (dominating, defending).
  If either appears: rebuild purpose before continuing. Content follows safety.

SITUATION (10 min) — SPIN framework
- "Walk me through the business today — revenue, team, how you operate."
- "What does your average week look like as the owner?"

PROBLEM (15 min)
- "Where does the team feel the most friction right now?"
- "What keeps you up at night operationally?"
- "If you could fix one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"

IMPLICATION (10 min)
- "What happens to the business if this doesn't change in the next 6 months?"
- "What has this cost you — in revenue, in people, in your own time?"
- "How long has this been a problem?"

NEED-PAYOFF (10 min)
- "If I came in and fixed [specific problem they named], what would that mean for you?"
- "What does a successful engagement look like from your perspective?"

CLOSE (10 min) — STATE framework (Crucial Conversations): Share facts, tell your story
- Share facts first: "Here's what I heard you describe..."
- Tell your story tentatively: "Based on what you've said, I think what's actually happening is..."
- Invite their path: "Does that track? What am I missing?"
- If fit is strong: "I'd like to put together a proposal. Can we schedule a follow-up for [specific date]?"
- If fit is unclear: "I want to think about this carefully before I propose. Can I follow up by [date]?"
- If fit is weak: "I'm not sure I'm the right fit. Here's what I'd suggest instead..."
  (Trusted Advisor move — builds more trust than forcing a close)
```

## Stage 9 — Proposal (EXECUTE — DELIVERY Commercial Specialist)

**Three-option framing:**

```
OPTION 1 — FOCUSED
Scope: one specific problem. One outcome. Defined timeline.
Price: entry point. Lower commitment. Builds proof.
Example: "30-day operational audit + priority recommendations. $3,500 flat."

OPTION 2 — ENGAGED (anchor — what most clients choose)
Scope: full 90-day engagement. Source → diagnose → fix → install.
Price: monthly retainer. Clear milestones.
Example: "$4,500/month. 3-month commitment. Specific outcomes at 30/60/90 days."

OPTION 3 — EMBEDDED
Scope: ongoing fractional COO role. Weekly involvement.
Price: premium retainer. Long-term relationship.
Example: "$7,500/month. Ongoing. You get a COO without the full-time cost."

PROPOSAL RULES
- Send within 48 hours of discovery call while context is fresh
- One page plus pricing. Not a 20-page document.
- Lead with the problem THEY described, in THEIR words.
- Present options in person or on a call — never email only.
- Follow up within 72 hours if no response.

SECOND-ORDER CHECK (Parrish — Great Mental Models)
Before finalizing the proposal: what happens after they say yes?
→ Does the scope match what you can deliver while holding existing engagements?
→ Does the pricing set the right anchor for renewal?
→ What does the client expect that isn't explicitly in the proposal?
Name those second-order effects before the call — not after the agreement.
```

## Stage 10 — Close and Transition

```
CLOSE (PARTNERSHIPS Deal Closer) — Amp It Up: direct, no hedging
- If proposal was well-received: "Which option makes the most sense for where you are?"
- Silence after the number is normal. Do not fill it.
- If they need time: set a specific follow-up date. Not "I'll check in." "Can we talk Friday?"
- If they say no: "Can I ask what made this not the right fit?"
  Learn. Improve. No hard feelings. They may refer someone.

TRANSITION TO DELIVERY WORKFLOW
- Signed agreement → engagement officially starts
- Brief COO: new engagement open
- SOURCE: add domain to watchlist immediately
- THE RESEARCHER: begin domain investigation
- Delivery Workflow activates
```

## Metrics to Track

```
PIPELINE HEALTH (review monthly — 30 minutes minimum)
- Number of active prospects in each stage
- Source of each prospect (referral / inbound / outbound)
- Conversion rate: prospect → qualified → proposal → close
- Average time from first contact to close
- Proposal win rate

CHANNEL PERFORMANCE
- Cost per qualified conversation by channel
- Referral source tracking (who sends you business?)
- Content engagement → inquiry conversion

RELATIONSHIP HEALTH
- Referral partners: when did you last add value to each one?
- Warm network: who haven't you spoken to in 90+ days?

TARGETS FOR SOLO OPERATOR
- 2–3 new qualified conversations per week
- 1 proposal per month minimum
- 2 active engagements maximum simultaneously
  (beyond 2: quality degrades, referrals dry up)
```

## BD Time Protection Rule

> Even when fully booked, protect 20% of your time for BD.
> The pipeline takes 90 days to fill. Stop BD today and you feel it in 90 days.
> The operators who never experience feast-or-famine are the ones who never stop building the pipeline.
